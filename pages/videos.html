Ex-machina (2014)
Ex-Machina (2014), a Sci-Fi Thriller directed by Alex Garland and running for 108 minutes, intricately weaves a tale around a programmer, Caleb, who is invited by his CEO, Nathan, to evaluate the human-like qualities of a highly advanced humanoid robot, Ava. The film scrutinizes philosophical and moral implications of artificial intelligence. It has been lauded for its provocative narrative and has received a score of 92% on Rotten Tomatoes.
Black Mirror (2011-)
Black Mirror (2011-), a British anthology series created by Charlie Brooker, explores the dark and often dystopian aspects of modern society and technology. Each episode stands alone, featuring different characters, settings, and realities, but all serve as a mirror reflecting the unforeseen consequences of new technological advancements. Known for its thought-provoking and cautionary tales, "Black Mirror" delves into the potential perils and moral dilemmas in a high-tech future, receiving critical acclaim and a Rotten Tomatoes score of 83%.
Her (2013)
Her (2013), a poignant Sci-Fi Drama by Spike Jonze, unfolds the tale of Theodore Twombly (Joaquin Phoenix), who, reeling from a breakup, forms an unprecedented bond with Samantha, an advanced operating system voiced by Scarlett Johansson. The film, lasting 126 minutes, navigates the intricate pathways of human connection and artificial intelligence, offering a contemplative look at love and loneliness. It has earned widespread acclaim, reflected in its 95% score on Rotten Tomatoes.
AlphaGo (2017)
AlphaGo (2017), a fascinating documentary directed by Greg Kohs, unveils the groundbreaking journey of the artificial intelligence program, AlphaGo, developed by DeepMind. The film captures the thrilling 2016 face-off between the AI and Go world champion, Lee Sedol, offering a profound look at the collision between man and machine in the ancient Chinese game of Go. It explores the complexities and potentials of AI, resonating deeply with tech enthusiasts and general audiences alike and earning widespread acclaim.
Steve Jobs (2015)
Steve Jobs (2015), a critically acclaimed Biographical Drama directed by Danny Boyle, portrays the riveting story of the iconic Apple co-founder, played by Michael Fassbender. Over three seminal product launches, the film delves into the visionary's tumultuous life, complex relationships, and relentless pursuit of technological excellence, showcasing his contributions to reshaping modern technology. With its sharp screenplay and compelling performances, the film has secured an 86% rating on Rotten Tomatoes.
Terminator (1984)
Terminator (1984), directed by James Cameron, is a groundbreaking Sci-Fi action film that revolutionized the genre. Arnold Schwarzenegger stars as the titular character, a relentless cyborg assassin sent back in time to terminate Sarah Connor, played by Linda Hamilton, whose future son will lead humanity in a war against machines. This film intertwines themes of fate, technology, and human resilience and remains a seminal work in cinema, noted for its innovative special effects and compelling narrative, with a Rotten Tomatoes score of 89% (Certified Fresh).
Gattaca (1997)
Gattaca (1997), a Sci-Fi drama directed by Andrew Niccol, presents a thought-provoking glimpse into a dystopian future where genetic engineering determines one’s life prospects. Ethan Hawke stars as Vincent, a man deemed inferior due to his natural birth, who assumes the identity of a genetically superior individual, played by Jude Law, to pursue his dream of space travel. The film delves into the ethical quandaries of genetic manipulation and the indomitable human spirit, earning accolades for its intelligent storytelling and rich thematic depth, reflected in its Rotten Tomatoes score of 82% (Certified Fresh).
Computer Science: The Crash Course
The Crash Course on Computer Science by Crash Course on YouTube is an enlightening series offering a comprehensive overview of the essentials of computer science. With an average episode length of about 12-15 minutes, this series delves deeply into the evolution of computers, the foundational principles of algorithms and data structures, and a myriad of programming paradigms and computational theories. It is meticulously designed to be accessible to beginners while remaining insightful for those with experience, making it a versatile learning resource.
CS50: Introduction to Computer Science by Harvard University
CS50, offered by Harvard University, stands as a seminal online course in computer science. It covers a variety of topics such as algorithms, software, and development, introducing students to the intricacies of programming languages like C, Python, and SQL. With engaging lectures and challenging problem sets, CS50 provides a robust and accessible platform for learning and exploring the world of computer science, suitable for beginners and those with prior experience.
